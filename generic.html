Living Curiously,
Learning Constantly

“You will see things and meet people and there is so much to learn from them.”
― Christopher Johnson McCandless, Into the Wild

My adult life could be characterized as a pursuit in squeezing the most out of each day. Since I was a child meandering around the Texas Panhandle I have believed that new experience is the zest of life. In this spirit I have attained a bachelors with honors in Biochemistry, taught myself various aspects of data specific programming, become a driven surfer, jeweler, and surfboard shaper all before the age of twenty-three. Following the completion of my undergraduate degree, I relocated to Oregon's Willamette Valley where I manage a small, but bustling local coffee shop. Amidst this I have carved out invaluable time building data-driven analytical skills as well as becoming a Pacific coastline regular immersing myself in the daily cinematic beauty of the region.

I hope to lean further into adulthood with a career that allows me to use my knowledge to aid others. Whether artistic or academic, I have an insatiable appetite for the opportunity to learn and improve upon the things I hold dearest to my heart. Despite a wealth of experiences under my belt, it feels as though I am standing at an exposition, eagerly awaiting what comes next.
